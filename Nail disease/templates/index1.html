{% if preds=="Acral_Lentiginous_Melanoma" %}

Symptoms

  • Dark streak under the nail (often brown or black)
  • Nail splitting or lifting
  • Bleeding or oozing from nail area
  • Swelling or pain in the nail bed
  • Changes in nail color or shape
  • Persistent nail lesion that does not heal

Causes

  • Uncontrolled growth of melanocytes (pigment cells)
  • Genetic mutations (e.g., KIT mutations)
  • Higher risk in people with darker skin tones
  • History of trauma or chronic irritation to the nail
  • Not typically related to UV exposure (unlike other melanomas)

Organs Infected

  • Primarily nails (subungual region)
  • Surrounding skin of fingers or toes
  • Nearby lymph nodes (if spread occurs)
  • Lungs (in advanced metastasis)
  • Liver and bones (late-stage cases)
  • Brain (rare but possible in advanced spread)

Treatment

  • Surgical removal of the tumor or affected nail
  • Amputation of part of the digit if deeply invasive
  • Sentinel lymph node biopsy for spread detection
  • Immunotherapy (e.g., pembrolizumab, nivolumab)
  • Targeted therapy (e.g., imatinib for KIT mutations)
  • Radiation therapy in non-surgical cases
{% endif %} {% if preds=="beau_s lines" %}

Symptoms

  • Horizontal grooves or depressions across the nail plate
  • Visible ridges that move forward as the nail grows
  • May affect one or multiple nails
  • Nail thinning or brittleness in severe cases
  • Discoloration near the grooves (sometimes)
  • Temporary halt in nail growth

Causes

  • Severe illness (e.g., high fever, infections)
  • Chemotherapy or radiation treatment
  • Uncontrolled diabetes or peripheral vascular disease
  • Zinc deficiency or malnutrition
  • Trauma or injury to the nail matrix
  • Systemic diseases (e.g., kidney or liver disease)

Organs Infected

  • Nails (fingernails and toenails)
  • Nail matrix (where nail growth begins)
  • Indirectly linked with systemic organs depending on cause (e.g., kidney, liver, immune system)

Treatment

  • Treat underlying medical condition (infection, diabetes, malnutrition)
  • Maintain proper nutrition (zinc, protein, vitamins)
  • Protect nails from trauma or pressure
  • Good nail hygiene and moisturizing
  • Medical management for systemic diseases
  • Grooves usually grow out naturally once the cause is resolved
{% endif %} {% if preds=="blue_finger" %}

Symptoms

  • Blue or bluish-purple discoloration of nails or fingertips
  • Coldness in fingers or toes
  • Numbness or tingling sensation
  • Pain or discomfort in affected fingers (sometimes)
  • Slow healing of small cuts or injuries
  • May appear suddenly or persistently depending on cause

Causes

  • Reduced oxygen supply in blood (peripheral cyanosis)
  • Poor circulation (Raynaud’s phenomenon, vascular disease)
  • Heart conditions (congenital heart disease, heart failure)
  • Lung diseases (COPD, pneumonia, asthma)
  • Exposure to extreme cold or frostbite
  • Certain medications or chemical exposure (rare)

Organs Infected

  • Nails and fingertips (directly visible)
  • Blood vessels (circulatory system)
  • Heart (if cardiovascular condition is the cause)
  • Lungs (if respiratory disorder is the cause)
  • Skin and peripheral tissues (due to low oxygen levels)

Treatment

  • Treat underlying cause (heart or lung disease management)
  • Improve circulation through warmth and exercise
  • Medications for blood flow (vasodilators in Raynaud’s)
  • Oxygen therapy in respiratory conditions
  • Smoking cessation to improve vascular health
  • Protect hands and feet from extreme cold
{% endif %} {% if preds=="clubbing" %}

Symptoms

  • Bulbous enlargement of the fingertips
  • Downward curving of the nails
  • Softening of the nail beds
  • Increased angle between nail and cuticle (>180°)
  • Spongy or floating sensation when pressing nail bed
  • May be painless but progressive over time

Causes

  • Chronic lung diseases (lung cancer, bronchiectasis, pulmonary fibrosis)
  • Heart diseases (congenital heart defects, endocarditis)
  • Inflammatory bowel diseases (Crohn’s, ulcerative colitis)
  • Liver cirrhosis and other chronic liver diseases
  • Thyroid disorders (rare)
  • Idiopathic (unknown cause in some cases)

Organs Infected

  • Nails and fingertips (directly visible)
  • Lungs (most common source of underlying disease)
  • Heart (cardiovascular involvement)
  • Liver (in cirrhosis and chronic liver conditions)
  • Digestive system (in IBD-related cases)
  • Endocrine system (rarely, in thyroid disorders)

Treatment

  • No direct cure for clubbing (focus on treating underlying condition)
  • Medications for lung/heart diseases (antibiotics, steroids, bronchodilators)
  • Surgery or chemotherapy/radiotherapy for lung cancer
  • Liver disease management (transplant in severe cases)
  • Treatment of inflammatory bowel disease (immunosuppressants, biologics)
  • Regular monitoring of nail and overall systemic health
{% endif %} {% if preds=="healthy" %}

Symptoms (Signs of Healthy Nails)

  • Smooth surface without pits or grooves
  • Consistent pinkish color with white tips
  • Uniform thickness and strong texture
  • No discoloration (yellow, blue, or black)
  • No swelling, redness, or pain around the nail
  • Steady growth at a normal rate

Causes (Factors Supporting Healthy Nails)

  • Balanced diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals
  • Proper hydration and good blood circulation
  • Regular nail hygiene and trimming
  • Protection from trauma or chemical exposure
  • Healthy lifestyle (exercise, no smoking)
  • Avoidance of frequent harsh nail treatments

Organs Involved

  • Nails (fingernails and toenails)
  • Nail matrix (responsible for nail growth)
  • Skin and cuticle surrounding the nail
  • Blood vessels (supply nutrients and oxygen)
  • Immune system (prevents infections)

Treatment / Prevention

  • Maintain a balanced diet (biotin, zinc, iron, vitamin E)
  • Keep nails clean, dry, and trimmed
  • Moisturize nails and cuticles regularly
  • Wear gloves when exposed to chemicals or water
  • Avoid biting or picking nails
  • Seek medical advice if sudden nail changes occur
{% endif %} {% if preds=="koilonychia" %}

Symptoms

  • Thin, brittle nails
  • Spoon-shaped depression in the center of the nail
  • Edges of nails turned upward
  • Nails break or split easily
  • Pale or whitish nail color (sometimes)
  • Can affect one nail or multiple nails

Causes

  • Iron deficiency anemia (most common cause)
  • Chronic blood loss (e.g., gastrointestinal bleeding)
  • Frequent exposure to petroleum-based products
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Heart disease (rarely)
  • Genetic predisposition in some families

Organs Infected

  • Nails (fingernails and toenails)
  • Nail matrix (responsible for nail growth)
  • Blood system (iron deficiency / anemia)
  • Thyroid gland (in hypothyroidism-related cases)
  • Cardiovascular system (in rare heart disease cases)

Treatment

  • Iron supplementation for anemia
  • Treat underlying causes of blood loss (e.g., ulcers, GI issues)
  • Balanced diet rich in iron, vitamin C, and protein
  • Treat thyroid or heart conditions if present
  • Avoid chemical exposure to nails
  • Nail care: trimming, moisturizing, protecting nails
{% endif %} {% if preds=="Onychogryphosis" %}

Symptoms

  • Thickened, overgrown, and curved nails (resembles a ram’s horn)
  • Yellowish-brown or opaque nail color
  • Pain or discomfort while walking (common in toenails)
  • Difficulty trimming nails due to hardness
  • Nail deformity often asymmetrical
  • Secondary infections around the nail (sometimes)

Causes

  • Repeated trauma or pressure on nails (tight shoes, injury)
  • Poor foot/nail hygiene
  • Peripheral vascular disease
  • Fungal infections (onychomycosis)
  • Neglect in elderly patients (common in toenails)
  • Genetic predisposition (rarely)

Organs Infected

  • Nails (mostly toenails, occasionally fingernails)
  • Nail bed and surrounding skin
  • Feet (pain and mobility issues)
  • Circulatory system (if vascular disease is underlying)
  • Skin barrier (risk of fungal or bacterial infection)

Treatment

  • Professional trimming and thinning of nails (podiatrist care)
  • Use of nail grinders or surgical clippers
  • Soaking nails to soften before cutting
  • Treat underlying fungal infections with antifungals
  • Corrective surgery for severe deformities
  • Proper footwear and foot hygiene to prevent recurrence
{% endif %} {% if preds=="onychomycosis" %}

Symptoms

  • Thickened nails
  • Yellow, brown, or white discoloration
  • Brittle, crumbly, or ragged nail edges
  • Distorted nail shape
  • Foul odor from the nail
  • Pain or discomfort in severe cases

Causes

  • Fungal infection by dermatophytes (e.g., Trichophyton species)
  • Yeasts (Candida species) or non-dermatophyte molds
  • Excessive sweating (hyperhidrosis)
  • Walking barefoot in damp communal areas (pools, locker rooms)
  • Reduced immunity (diabetes, HIV, old age)
  • History of athlete’s foot (tinea pedis)

Organs Infected

  • Nails (toenails more commonly than fingernails)
  • Nail bed and surrounding skin
  • Feet (if associated with athlete’s foot)
  • Immune system indirectly (in immunocompromised patients)

Treatment

  • Oral antifungal medications (terbinafine, itraconazole)
  • Topical antifungal creams, lacquers, or solutions
  • Nail debridement (thinning and trimming)
  • Laser therapy for resistant cases
  • Good foot hygiene (keeping feet dry and clean)
  • Preventive measures: avoid sharing shoes/nail tools
{% endif %} {% if preds=="pitting" %}

Symptoms

  • Small depressions or pits on the nail surface
  • Irregular nail texture
  • Discoloration (yellow-brown or opaque spots)
  • Nail splitting or crumbling in severe cases
  • May affect one or multiple nails
  • Associated with other skin/joint symptoms if systemic cause exists

Causes

  • Psoriasis (most common cause)
  • Alopecia areata (autoimmune hair loss condition)
  • Eczema and other chronic dermatitis
  • Reactive arthritis
  • Connective tissue diseases
  • Occasional trauma to nail matrix

Organs Infected

  • Nails (fingernails and toenails)
  • Nail matrix (where nail formation occurs)
  • Skin (in psoriasis and eczema cases)
  • Hair (in alopecia areata-related cases)
  • Joints (in psoriatic or reactive arthritis)
  • Immune system (autoimmune-related diseases)

Treatment

  • Treat underlying condition (psoriasis, eczema, arthritis)
  • Topical corticosteroids or vitamin D analogs for nail psoriasis
  • Systemic therapies (methotrexate, biologics in severe cases)
  • Moisturizers to protect nail surface
  • Nail care: gentle trimming, avoid trauma
  • Phototherapy (UV treatment) in resistant cases
{% endif %} {% if preds=="psoriasis" %}

Symptoms

  • Pitting (tiny depressions on the nail surface)
  • Discoloration (yellow-brown spots, oil-drop appearance)
  • Thickening of nails
  • Nail crumbling or splitting
  • Onycholysis (separation of nail from nail bed)
  • Associated pain or tenderness

Causes

  • Autoimmune reaction where immune system attacks nail cells
  • Genetic predisposition (family history of psoriasis)
  • Psoriasis of skin spreading to nail matrix and nail bed
  • Psoriatic arthritis (joint inflammation linked with nail disease)
  • Environmental triggers (stress, injury, infection)
  • Immune dysregulation with chronic inflammation

Organs Infected

  • Nails (fingernails and toenails)
  • Nail matrix and nail bed
  • Skin (in psoriasis plaques)
  • Joints (in psoriatic arthritis)
  • Immune system (autoimmune dysfunction)

Treatment

  • Topical corticosteroids or vitamin D analogs
  • Intralesional corticosteroid injections (in severe nail disease)
  • Systemic therapies (methotrexate, cyclosporine)
  • Biologic agents (etanercept, adalimumab, ustekinumab)
  • Phototherapy (UV light treatment)
  • Proper nail care: trimming, moisturizing, avoiding trauma
{% endif %} {% if preds=="terry_s nail" %}

Symptoms

  • Nails appear white with a “ground-glass” look
  • Pink or reddish-brown band near the nail tips
  • Loss of normal lunula (half-moon shape at nail base)
  • Uniform discoloration across most nails
  • Usually affects all fingernails, sometimes toenails
  • No pain or direct nail damage (cosmetic sign)

Causes

  • Liver cirrhosis (most common cause)
  • Chronic heart failure
  • Kidney failure (chronic renal disease)
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Aging (can occur in elderly without disease)
  • Systemic illnesses affecting circulation and nail bed

Organs Infected

  • Nails (fingernails more than toenails)
  • Liver (cirrhosis, chronic liver disease)
  • Heart (chronic heart failure)
  • Kidneys (chronic renal disease)
  • Endocrine system (diabetes-related changes)
  • Circulatory system (altered blood supply in nail bed)

Treatment

  • No direct treatment for nails — focus on underlying cause
  • Liver disease management (antivirals, transplant in severe cases)
  • Heart failure therapy (medications, lifestyle modifications)
  • Kidney failure treatment (dialysis, transplant)
  • Blood sugar control in diabetes
  • Healthy lifestyle (diet, exercise, avoiding alcohol/smoking)
{% endif %} {% if preds=="white nail" %}

Symptoms

  • White spots or streaks on the nail plate
  • Entire nail may appear white (total leukonychia)
  • Partial whitening in bands or patches
  • Can affect one or multiple nails
  • Usually painless and harmless in mild cases
  • Severe cases linked with systemic disease

Causes

  • Minor nail trauma or injury
  • Nail infections (fungal, bacterial)
  • Zinc or calcium deficiency
  • Systemic diseases (cirrhosis, chronic kidney disease)
  • Chemotherapy or medications
  • Genetic leukonychia (rare inherited condition)

Organs Infected

  • Nails (nail plate discoloration)
  • Liver (cirrhosis-related whitening)
  • Kidneys (chronic renal failure)
  • Blood (anemia, malnutrition)
  • Immune system (weakened by systemic illness)
  • Skin/nail bed (fungal or bacterial infection)

Treatment

  • Usually no treatment needed for minor cases
  • Maintain good nail hygiene
  • Treat underlying infections (antifungal, antibiotics)
  • Correct nutritional deficiencies (zinc, protein, calcium)
  • Manage systemic illnesses (liver, kidney, anemia)
  • Avoid excessive nail trauma (trimming, manicure safety)
{% endif %} {% if preds=="yellow nails" %}

Symptoms

  • Thickened, yellowish nails
  • Slow nail growth
  • Increased nail curvature
  • Nail detachment from nail bed (onycholysis)
  • Swelling in legs due to lymphedema
  • Respiratory symptoms (chronic cough, pleural effusion)

Causes

  • Yellow Nail Syndrome (rare systemic condition)
  • Fungal infections (onychomycosis)
  • Chronic respiratory diseases (bronchiectasis, sinusitis)
  • Lymphedema (lymphatic obstruction)
  • Liver disease or kidney disease
  • Side effects of medications (e.g., tetracyclines)

Organs Infected

  • Nails (discoloration and thickening)
  • Lungs (chronic lung disease, pleural effusion)
  • Lymphatic system (lymphedema of legs/arms)
  • Immune system (associated with chronic infection)
  • Liver and kidneys (systemic involvement)
  • Skin/nail bed (secondary fungal infection)

Treatment

  • Treat underlying cause (lung disease, lymphedema, infection)
  • Antifungal therapy for fungal nail infections
  • Vitamin E supplementation (sometimes beneficial)
  • Manual lymphatic drainage and compression for lymphedema
  • Respiratory therapy for chronic lung conditions
  • Nail care: trimming, protection, avoid trauma
